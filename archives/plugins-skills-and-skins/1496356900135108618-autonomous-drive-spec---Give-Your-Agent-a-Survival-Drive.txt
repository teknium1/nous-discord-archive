    Think of it like a chef in a restaurant.
    
    When there are orders, the chef cooks. That's the normal job.

    - Wipe down the station (so nothing breaks mid-rush)
    - Sharpen the knives (so they work better next time)  
    - Check what's in the fridge (so they know what's available)
    
    That's all this is. When your AI agent has nothing to do, instead of sitting idle, it:
    1. Checks its own tools are working (backups, services alive, etc.)

    3. Updates its knowledge — scans for new info, refreshes what it knows
    
    Your tasks always come first. The "between-orders" stuff pauses instantly when you show up.
