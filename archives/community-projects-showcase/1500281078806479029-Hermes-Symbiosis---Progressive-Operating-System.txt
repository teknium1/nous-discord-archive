[2026-05-04T15:43:28.543000+00:00] colmeio_66868 (id=1500885584888205556)
    I’ve been experimenting with making Hermes-Orchestrator itself part of the runtime.
    
    Instead of just running agents, the repo now guides how agents evolve it —
    but with strict constraints:
    
    - prompt-level guardrails to prevent unsafe mutations
    - extension-first policy (plugins > core edits)
    - doc-sync enforced so reality stays consistent
    - resumable roadmap so changes happen in controlled steps
    
    The key problem I’m trying to solve:
    
    If agents can extend the system, how do you prevent them from corrupting it?
    
    Right now the approach is:
    constrain evolution through policy, structure, and small verified steps —
    not unrestricted autonomy.
    
    Still early, but it’s starting to feel like a system where agents can safely
    *participate in its evolution* without breaking it.
    
    Curious how others are thinking about safe long-horizon system evolution.

    WIS is the first vertical slice of wasm-agent as an agent-native browser substrate.
    
    Instead of embedding apps through iframes or relying on a backend, WIS creates a local, sandboxed interaction surface where UI, state, events, permissions, and automation hooks live together inside the artifact.
    
    That means an agent does not need to “look at the screen” like a human.
    It can inspect the surface as structured state, act on it through defined events, and carry the whole environment across spaces.
    
    This turns wasm-agent from a UI shell into a portable execution surface:
    share a space,
    fork an app,
    automate the interface,
    and let the agent operate inside it.
    
    The first goal is not to build a full browser.
    The goal is to prove the primitive:
    a backendless, non-iframe, browser-like surface that can render, react, expose state, and travel as a shareable agentic artifact.
    
    
    ## WIS: Web/WASM Interaction Surface

    It is not an iframe and not a backend-rendered app.
