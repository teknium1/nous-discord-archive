    Hey everyone,
    
    I've been exploring what I see as a core limitation in standard Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) models: **the experts work in complete isolation.** To address this, I've been prototyping a new architecture I'm calling **Mixture of Collaborative Experts (MoC)**, and I would be honored to get feedback from this community.
    
    The core concept is to shift from a system where experts work alone to one where they form a "task force" to collaborate on a solution *before* producing a final, synthesized output.
    
    ---
    
     How It Works (The TL;DR)
    
    The MoC architecture introduces two key innovations to the standard FFN block:
    
    1.  **Contextual Routing:** Before selecting experts, the router first applies `self-attention` over the initial outputs from **all** experts. This allows it to make an informed, context-aware decision about which experts form the best team for a given token.
    
    2.  **Collaborative Fusion:** The `k` selected experts then use a `cross-attention` mechanism to "talk" to each other, allowing them to refine their individual outputs based on their peers' perspectives.

     Discussion & Feedback
    
    I'm particularly interested in hearing your thoughts on:
    *   Potential failure modes or training instabilities you might foresee with this approach.
    *   The auxiliary loss I've proposed, which combines expert load balancing with a "diversity loss" on the collaboration's attention weights.
    *   Any ideas for more efficient implementations of the collaborative fusion step.
