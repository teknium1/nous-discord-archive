    hey! I just open-sourced NanoIndex, an agentic RAG harness built for long financial and legal documents. thought this community would find the architecture interesting.
    
    most RAG systems fail on long docs because they get extraction wrong before retrieval even starts. flatten a 200-page report into chunks and you lose the structure the answer depends on.
    
    NanoIndex indexes documents as trees and builds domain-specific entity graphs in a single indexing call. the agentic harness navigates structure at query time: breaks the question into required data points, reads the document outline, retrieves in multiple rounds, checks sufficiency, verifies arithmetic before returning anything.
    
    two modes:
    - fast mode: entity graph traversal, 2 LLM calls, queries in 10 seconds
    - agentic mode: full tree navigation, multi-round retrieval, sufficiency checks
    
    95% on FinanceBench. 96% on DocBench Legal.
    
    benchmarks are verifiable. the demo shows the actual questions, retrieved tree nodes, and ground truth side by side.
    
    demo: https://nanoindex.nanonets.com/demo
    github: https://github.com/NanoNets/nanoindex
    
    happy to get into the agent architecture if anyone's curious.
