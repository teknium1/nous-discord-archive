    This is raw data from measurements I took on IBM's newest quantum computers (Heron r1 and r2, with 156&133 qubits). I specifically ran experiments creating a 100-qubit GHZ state (a type of quantum entanglement). The output bitstrings include real quantum randomness plus noise from the hardware (like decoherence and gate errors).
    My idea: Adding this "true randomness" during the model's fine-tuning stage works like a data-based way to add helpful noise. It appears to stop the model from getting stuck (mode collapse). Hermes 3 was the ideal starting model for this test because it's reliable and strong!
