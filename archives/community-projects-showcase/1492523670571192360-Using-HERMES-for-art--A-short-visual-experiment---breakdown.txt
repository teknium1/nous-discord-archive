    Here is a quick look at the philosophy and history behind the piece.
    
    ### ABOUT THE PIECE
    The video opens with a simple terminal command: `"$ Tell me about the Macy Conferences."` This is a representative query fed into Hermes.
    
    While the query demonstrates a standard human-AI interaction, the subject itself (the Macy Conferences) traces the historical lineage of how we ended up in this situation. It looks at the history that gave us these tools. Throughout the mid-20th century, the science of Cybernetics redefined humans, machines, and society as identical systems governed by feedback and control. Today, much of our digital (and physical) infrastructure is built on this exact assumption. It treats users as biological machines emitting signals to be measured and contained.
    
    This piece is about the dangers of those closed, black-box systems. Hermes appears at the end as a tool designed to break the cage. By giving users the open framework to build the tools themselves, we return some much needed agency to the individual. It lets us leverage the power of agents without feeding into systems designed to manage us (if you're willing to use local models, but we keep the idea for dramatic effect, artistic license lol).
