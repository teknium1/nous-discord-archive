    I built a plugin that lets Hermes think while you're away.
    
    Dream Auto indexes your sessions, grades them for "dream potential," and runs MCTS-powered background reasoning jobs when your machine is idle. The insights get injected into your active context automatically — no disruption, just smarter responses.
    
    Key bits:
    - Error-triggered dreams — tool crashes auto-queue troubleshooting so you don't fix the same bug twice
    - Resource-aware — only runs when CPU/RAM are free
    - Zero hardcoded thresholds — the LLM decides what's worth thinking about
    - Cross-platform — Linux, macOS, WSL
    
    Install in 10 seconds:
    Paste this into your Hermes chat:
    
    `Go to https://github.com/StefanIsMe/dream-auto/blob/main/SETUP.md and follow the agent installation steps to install the Dream Auto plugin on this machine.`
    
    Hermes clones the repo, installs everything, registers cron jobs, and reports back. Or run python3 install.py manually if you prefer.
    
    Repo: https://github.com/StefanIsMe/dream-auto
    
    Feedback and PRs welcome.
    
    ---
    
    I know this is still early and there's more to do — the MCTS engine hits occasional crashes, the queue can back up, and I want to add more insight categories to the dashboard. But I think it's cool, it works, and I wanted to share it with the community rather than polish it in private. If you try it, let me know what breaks and what you'd want it to do.

    Dream Auto v3 is live.
    
    Four days ago it was a v1 prototype with sequential rollouts, basic UCB1, and no protection against dreams that spin forever. Here is what changed.
    
    BEFORE: Sequential rollouts.
    
    Every MCTS iteration ran one step at a time. Rollout. MetaRAG lookup. Another rollout. Another lookup. A 15-second lookup meant the whole tree waited.
    
    AFTER: Parallel everything.
    
    MetaRAG lookups and rollouts fire simultaneously. A 20-minute dream now finishes in under 4.
    
    BEFORE: Basic UCB1.
    
    Standard Monte Carlo selection. Treats a 90% win-rate branch the same as an 80% win-rate branch even when the 80% has been tried three times and the 90% has been tried once.
    
    AFTER: UCB1-Tuned with CI-width bonus.
    
    Variance-aware. High-variance nodes get exploration space instead of being locked by lucky rollouts. Uncertain nodes get extra nudge, scaled by depth.
    
    BEFORE: No staleness detection.
    
    Dreams could spin for hours and produce nothing.
    
    AFTER: Staleness detection and wallclock killer.
    
    20+ minutes with no tree growth and the dream cuts off. Any dream over 30 minutes is killed globally. No orphaned processes.
    
    BEFORE: O(n) database queries.
    
    Draining the queue meant a full scan. Sorting sessions by potential meant reading every row.
    
    AFTER: SQLite indexes on all frequently-queried columns.
    
    Queue drains and session sorts are O(log n). The scheduler stays fast as the index grows.
    
    BEFORE: Redundant knowledge lookups.
    
    Every MetaRAG call re-fetched the same topics.
    
    AFTER: Knowledge cache DB.
    
    Caches by topic with hashing. Repeated queries hit cache.
    
    BEFORE: Every hook fired every turn.
    
    Trivial questions triggered the full pipeline.
    
    AFTER: Throttle mechanism.
    
    Hook fires at most every 5 turns. Simple questions route to fast-path. Complex sessions get full MCTS.
    
    Before v3, Dream Auto was background noise. Now it learns from your errors and delivers findings before you ask the next question.
    
    Already running Dream Auto? Upgrade: github.com/StefanIsMe/dream-auto

    Module shadowing is a technique where you clone a especific module, patch it, benchmark it, against current module in a loop. Once that module wins in the benchmark you replace de old module. So you create a shadow and try to improve by patching it, if works shadow becomes main, otherwise you just destruct it. So you can take the most inefficient steps of the day and reflect on that while dreaming so once wakeup the agent turned more efficient and you can know by how much and where the improvement were made
